# SDAH Blog Refinement — Voice and Structure (2026-03-17)

**Project:** sandiegoamazinghomes  
**Scope:** All 14 articles in `content/blog/*.md`

## Goal

Make every blog article feel like it was written by the same real San Diego local: natural, confident, slightly conversational, not templated or repetitive.

## Changes applied

1. **Removed AI-style structure**
   - Dropped obvious section headers (e.g. "The Reality:", "Practical Guidance", "Bottom Line", "San Diego–Specific Breakdown").
   - Content reflowed into continuous prose with varied paragraph length and transitions.

2. **Removed generic language**
   - Cut or rewrote filler (e.g. "In today's market…", "San Diego is a vibrant coastal city…").
   - Tightened intros and conclusions.

3. **Flow and voice**
   - Varied sentence length; mixed short and medium paragraphs.
   - One consistent voice across posts: grounded, practical, not salesy, slightly conversational.

4. **Internal linking**
   - Each article references, where natural: [market page](/market), [neighborhoods](/neighborhoods) or [neighborhood profiles](/neighborhoods), and [home search](/homes) (avoiding "listings" in link text).

5. **Unified ending**
   - Every article now ends with this block:
     - "If you're trying to figure out where you fit in this market, it helps to look at: • current trends • how different neighborhoods compare • what's actually available. That's usually where things start to come together."

6. **San Diego specificity**
   - Kept real neighborhood names (North Park, Clairemont, La Mesa, Encinitas, Chula Vista, Mission Valley, Eastlake, etc.) and real pricing context where it was already present.
