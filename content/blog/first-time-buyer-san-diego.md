Buying your first home in San Diego County is a big step. Here's what actually matters.

Before you fall in love with a place, get pre-approved with a lender. Pre-approval shows sellers you're serious and tells you exactly what you can afford. In a competitive market, offers without pre-approval often get passed over. Shop rates with at least one local lender and one credit union or national lender so you can compare.

San Diego isn't one market—it's many. Coastal areas like La Jolla and Del Mar command premium prices. For first-time buyers, neighborhoods like Mission Valley, Chula Vista, Eastlake, and Bonita often offer more square footage and easier entry while still putting you within reach of the coast and job centers. [Neighborhood profiles](/neighborhoods) can walk you through livability, commute times, and school districts so you choose a fit that works for your budget and lifestyle.

When inventory is tight, be ready to act quickly. That means pre-approval in hand, a clear must-have list, and flexibility on closing dates when it makes sense. In some areas listing prices are just a starting point; in others there's more room to negotiate. Your agent can help you read the local data—days on market, price reductions, and comparable sales—so you know when to move fast and when you have leverage.

That's usually where things start to come together.
