# April 2026 — San Diego Amazing Homes

Monthly market intelligence for San Diego County homes and neighborhoods.

## Market Snapshot

This month the San Diego County market shows a median sold price of **$950K** with **1,200** active listings and an average of **28** days on market. Mortgage rates are at **6.75%**, influencing both buyer budgets and seller expectations.

Net in-migration from LA and Bay Area; outflows to Arizona and Nevada for affordability. San Diego remains a top destination for coastal and lifestyle relocations.

## Hot Neighborhoods

Neighborhoods drawing the most buyer traffic and quick moves this month:

- **La Jolla** — Oceanfront village feel with coves, cliffs, and a strong sense of community.
- **Del Mar** — Upscale coastal village with the fairgrounds, beaches, and a low-key resort feel.
- **Rancho Santa Fe** — Upscale North County enclave with estates, trails, and a quiet, private feel.

## Price Trends

The median sits in the upper tier; competition remains strong in entry-level and move-up segments.

We expect the usual seasonal lift into spring and summer, with the rate environment and inventory levels driving the pace of sales.

## Luxury Market

Strong median prices reflect sustained demand in the luxury tier; La Jolla, Del Mar, Rancho Santa Fe, and Coronado remain core luxury markets. Days on market have stayed relatively tight, indicating that well-positioned luxury properties are still moving.

Cash and jumbo financing continue to play a large role; we recommend working with a local lender who knows the high-end market.

## First-Time Buyer Insights

First-time buyers in San Diego face a competitive but navigable market. Rates are a key factor in monthly payments; locking when you find the right home can help with budgeting. Inventory has improved, so there are more options to compare and less pressure to waive contingencies.
